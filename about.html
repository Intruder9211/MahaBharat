About Mahabharat

Mahabharat is one of the two major Sanskrit epics of ancient India, the other being the Ramayana. It narrates the conflict between the Pandavas and the Kauravas, two branches of a royal family in the kingdom of Hastinapur. The story spans over 100,000 shlokas (verses) and is divided into 18 books, each containing several chapters.

Mahabharat is considered a masterpiece of world literature and has been translated into numerous languages. Its themes include love, politics, war, ethics, and spirituality. Along with the Ramayana, it has played a major role in shaping Indian culture and identity.

Our website aims to provide a comprehensive resource for anyone interested in Mahabharat. We offer summaries of each book, character profiles, analysis of key themes, and more.
